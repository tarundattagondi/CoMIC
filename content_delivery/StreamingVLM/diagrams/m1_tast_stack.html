Why Each Block Matters

M1 (DyMU): kills intra-frame spatial redundancy — same-object patches collapse before scoring.

STAMP-T: picks the temporally-salient subset; entropy-adaptive r adjusts to scene complexity.

TAST: adds 32 EMA-accumulated state tokens that carry long-range context the per-chunk top-k would otherwise drop.

r=0.30: aggressive ratio that holds accuracy on short-to-medium video; falls behind only on multi-minute plot reasoning.

★ vs. Prior Work

FastV / FasterVLM: spatial only, LLM attention, no temporal memory.

PruneVid: feature cosine, no learned salience.

DyCoke: KV-cache merge in LLM prefill, not at ViT.

STAMP-T (single): our temporal-only ablation, no DyMU merge or TAST state.

Ours: spatial merge + temporal scoring + accumulative state — all training-free, all composable.

Headline Results (paired bootstrap, 95% CI)

Cross-model DeViBench (Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct, n=652): 86.50%, Δ +4.42pp [+1.82, +6.88] vs StreamingVLM-vanilla 82.08% — sig. win, training-free transfer

StreamingVLM same-model DeViBench (n=652): 82.21% [79.14, 85.12] — matches vanilla at ~30% LLM tokens (compute-equivalence)
